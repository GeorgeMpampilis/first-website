Steve Aoki At UK In April!

Kicking off 2014 with a bang, the return of the Grammy nominated Electro/House DJ & Producer Steve Aoki hits UK shores in April for his biggest shows to date. Steve’s acrobatic crowd surfing stunts, cake throwing and general dancefloor debauchery has made him renowned as one of the most energetic and entertaining dance artists on the circuit and we can no doubt expect the same fun and frolics and huge stage production at his forthcoming UK dates in April creating what is set to be one of this year’s unmissable shows.

WHEN: April 4, 2014 @ 9:00 pm – April 5, 2014 @ 3:00 am

WHERE: O2 Academy Brixton 211 Stockwell Road London SW9 9SL UK

Steve Aoki And Hardwell Among Names For Mmysteryland Festival

Mysteryland, the world's longest-running festival, today reveals the first phase of its most creative and diverse line-up yet, which features Hardwell, Steve Aoki, Dimitri Vegas & Like Mike and Chris Liebing. Other artists heading to the Netherlands on August 23 at the site in Hoofddorp include Loco Dice, twin duo NERVO, Art Department and dubstep demon Rusko. This year, Mysteryland has three open-air stages offering a wide range of techno, tech house, deep house and even some tropical and hip hop beats. More names will be announced at the end of April and ticket sales start at 10am on April 5. Head here for more information.

Steve Aoki Discusses 'Neon Future' Album & Moving To Las Vegas: Watch The Ultra Interview

Steve Aoki is one of the many electro-dance stars invading Miami this weekend for the 2014 Ultra Music Festival, but the veteran producer and founder of Dim Mak Records is preparing an ambitious double-album that will set him apart from his peers. Watch Aoki discuss the project, along with moving from Los Angeles to Las Vegas, with Billboard's Kerri Mason at the Red Bull Guest House below:

Aoki says that his forthcoming project, titled "Neon Future," will be released in two halves, with the first half featuring artists like Fall Out Boy, Empire of the Sun and Waka Flocka Flame. "It's two albums, and the first part is coming out in August, and the second part is coming out in 2015," says Aoki, who describes Part 2 as "deeper" than the first installment.
